Tentacles of ‘0ktapus’ Threat Group Victimize 130 Firms

Severity: 8/10

A massive phishing campaign, dubbed "0ktapus," targeted over 130 organizations, resulting in 9,931 compromised accounts. The campaign spoofed Okta's multi-factor authentication system, tricking employees into submitting identity credentials and MFA codes. The attacks, which affected 114 US-based firms and 68 other countries, are considered highly successful and may have been triggered by initial targeting of telecommunications companies.

Claude Mythos Has Found 271 Zero-Days in Firefox

Severity: 10 Zero-day vulnerabilities are considered severe as they can be exploited immediately, without the vendor having any prior knowledge or warning, giving attackers a significant advantage. The discovery of 271 zero-days in Firefox significantly increases the risk to users, making it a high-priority issue in the cybersecurity landscape./10

The Firefox team used AI to scan its browser, identifying 271 security vulnerabilities, many of which would have posed significant threats in 2025. The collaboration with Anthropic and utilization of Claude Mythos Preview allowed for rapid fixes in the latest Firefox release (150), marking a hopeful shift in the balance between defenders and attackers, favoring those able to patch and quickly deploy fixes.
